CoderDojo Ennis

Covid Risk Management Procedures

Last updated: 22th September 2021

Covid Risk Manager: Phil Miesle

Background

Coder Dojo Ennis was established in March of 2012, originally hosted at St Flannans but at Ennis CBS since 2015. Our Coder Dojo is one of the largest in Ireland with 50-60 in attendance each week, and with a waiting list sometimes reaching nearly 100 students. Children are able to learn computer coding and related skills at their own pace; our mentors work to help children teach and inspire each other more than dispensing wisdom from the front of a classroom (children get enough of that during the week!) For more information about Coder Dojo Ennis, please visit  https://coderdojoennis.com/

Since Covid 19 restrictions in March of 2019, our Dojo quickly pivoted to “Online Dojo.”  While it was great to be able to provide some continuity, our weekly attendance dropped by over half, with typically 15-20 students in attendance. The reasons for this are varied, but from an outcome perspective, those students who attended online dojo regularly were not getting the same quality of interactions and inspiration from their fellow students.

We have very much been looking forward to a return of “in-person Dojo”, but of course wish to do this in as safe a manner as we possibly can. As we are based in a newly-built school, we have the advantage of large classrooms, good ventilation, interactive whiteboards that allow the mentors to teach as much as possible from the “top” of the classroom, and even CO2 monitors in every room.


All these advantages notwithstanding, we must also have established procedures and protocols for managing the risk of Covid transmission, particularly as most of our children are of primary school age and remain unvaccinated. The remainder of this document outlines our policies and procedures. Each parent will be given a copy of these procedures, which will be continually updated to keep current with public health advice.

Attendance Management

  • Only those people who are signed up as members will be invited to attend
  • We will use EventBrite ticket management software, requiring all attendees to sign up each week in advance of attendance, and used to track who is in attendance each week
  • Each week, we will ask for confirmation of the following for each attendee; if any are answered “yes” we will contact the individual and ask not to attend:
  1. Does the attendee have symptoms of cough, fever, high temperature, sore throat, runny nose, breathlessness or flu like symptoms now or in the past 10 days?
  2. Has the attendee been diagnosed with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 infection in the last 10 days?
  3. Has the attendee been in close contact of a person who is confirmed or suspected case of COVID-19 in the past 10 days (i.e. less than 2m for more than 15 minutes accumulative in 1 day)

Reducing Transmission Risk - PPE

  • Hand sanitiser will be provided at point of entry, and all attendees are expected to clean their hands at this point
  • Face masks to be worn at all times by adults and those attending secondary school
  • All surfaces used during each session will be cleaned with sanitising fluid at the end of each session, including but not limited to tables, chairs, door handles
  • Attendees are expected to bring their own computer; the dojo will provide rental laptops for the term to those who do not have a computer available to use
  • Room CO2 meter to be monitored continuously. If not “green” windows and/or doors to be opened to facilitate more rapid air changes. If “red” for more than 10 minutes, the room is to be emptied until it returns to “green”, with those in the room asked to go outdoors for at least 10 minutes.

Reducing Transmission Risk - Social Distancing

  • Parents of children 13+ will be asked to remain off-site if possible.
  • Parents of children aged 9-12 must remain on-site but will be asked to congregate in the library and observe social distancing
  • Parents of children under the age of 9 will be asked to remain in the classrooms
  • The number of people in each classroom will be limited to no more than 28 people:
  1. 24 members/parents seated at desks
  2. 1-3 technical mentors
  3. 1-2 room monitors
  • Room assignments and seating to remain as “fixed” as possible; a seating plan will be devised and is expected to be followed at all times
  • One technical mentor to float between rooms
  • One Covid manager to float between rooms

Close Contact Communication / Coordination

  • In the event the Dojo is contacted by a Contact Tracer, the Covid Risk Manager will coordinate with the Contact Tracer to determine:
  1. What contacts need to be designated as a “close contact”
  2. List of necessary contact details to be provided to the Contact Tracer
  • In the event the Dojo is contacted by a member who has been deemed a “close contact” or has tested positive, the Covid Risk Manager will contact the HSE in order to determine the course of action.
  • In all circumstances, the Covid Risk Manager will connect with CBS designated point of contact to inform of the situation, and to undertake any such actions as requested by CBS.
  • Specific communications will be determined by the HSE Contact Tracing team, and carried out with minimal risk of exposing personal information.
